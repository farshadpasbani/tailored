Your posting reads like a description of how I already work. You want someone who treats a language model as a component to be constrained and measured rather than a magic box to be trusted on faith, and that posture is the through line of everything I have built for the last three years.

On guardrails. At Meridian Labs I lead the platform team that owns the layer every customer-facing feature ships behind. In the open I built Gatehouse, a deterministic policy layer that checks model output against schema, style, and safety rules before it reaches a user, which turns flaky behaviour into a build-time failure rather than a production incident.

On measurement. I am a strong believer that a model change should be judged on a number, not a hunch. My Lighthouse harness scores every prompt or model swap against the same frozen fixtures and blocks the merge when answer quality regresses, so the team argues about evidence instead of vibes.

On the unhappy path. Your note about making failures loud in continuous integration rather than quiet in production is exactly the discipline I bring. I design the empty, malformed, and over-limit cases first, because those are the inputs that decide whether a system is trustworthy.

I would welcome the chance to talk about how I would help Halyard ship language-model features that behave. My public work is the clearest evidence of how I think, and I am happy to walk you through any of it.
